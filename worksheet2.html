Worksheet 2: Information Interpretation

Question 1

An online tech-journal yesterday released an audio-only conversation with an individual using the handle "NullPrime." NullPrime alleged that a well-known smart-speaker manufacturer had quietly stored fragments of users' private dialogue to tune its voice-recognition models. When a leading documentary network requested an on-camera follow-up, the source agreed only on condition that both face and legal name remain concealed; the network declined these terms, so no video interview occurred. Within hours, influential gadget bloggers launched threads portraying the accusations as mostly conjecture and praising the firm's transparency record. SocialBee Analytics' sentiment dashboard later showed public opinion trending modestly pro-company, though a vocal minority demanded evidence. Late last night, the National Data-Protection Commission cited Section 47 emergency powers to open a formal inquiry into the matter.

1. The source wished to keep their true identity undisclosed.

2. Nearly the entire online audience immediately dismissed NullPrime's claims.

3. The smart-speaker company is explicitly described as globally famous.

4. NullPrime is stated to have been a senior employee of the company.

5. Government regulators have initiated an official examination of the allegations.
